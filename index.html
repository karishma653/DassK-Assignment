Welcome to Trinidad and Tobago's Food

From doubles on the street corner to Sunday callaloo, let's discover the heart of Trini culture through food. These are some of the most beloved and popular dishes in Trinidad and Tobago.


Popular Trinidadian Dishes


  1. Doubles

    Doubles is popular street food that is made with two baras (flat fried bread) and filled with channa (curried chick peas). There are also topped with mango chutney, bandanya, cucumber, tamarind and pepper sauce!

  2. Bake & Shark

    Bake and Shark is a distinctive Trini dish. It is known for its delicious combination of fried bread (bake) and stuffed with fried, seasoned shark meat. It is usually topped with lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers and pineapple. Condiments like mustard, ketchup, mayo, pepper sauce, chadon beni and tamarind sauce are also used to dress the dish.

  3. Callaloo

    Bake and Shark is a distinctive Trini dish. It is known for its delicious combination of fried bread (bake) and stuffed with fried, seasoned shark meat. It is usually topped with lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers and pineapple. Condiments like mustard, ketchup, mayo, pepper sauce, chadon beni and tamarind sauce are also used to dress the dish.

  4. Roti

    There are different types of Roti (flatbread) but one of the most popular trini roti is Chicken Roti. Which is basically curried/stewed chicken and potatoes wrapped in a dhalpuri roti similar to a burrito.

  5. Pelau

    Pelau is a savory one-pot Trinidadian favorite made with pigeon peas, chicken (or beef), coconut milk and caramelized sugar. It is delicately seasoned with Caribbean green seasoning, fresh herbs & spices, and simmered in coconut milk until it reaches perfection. It is usually served with a side of coleslaw and fresh salad.
